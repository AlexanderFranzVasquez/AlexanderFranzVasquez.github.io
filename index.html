About Me

I am currently pursuing a B.S. in Mechatronics Engineering at PUCP. My academic and personal projects focus on the modeling, design, and control of robotic systems, particularly aerial platforms and legged robots. I am especially interested in control-oriented modeling and advanced control techniques with real-world implementation.

Selected Projects

Hybrid Ground–Aerial Robot (ATMO-Inspired)

Course project focused on the mechanical design and control considerations of a transformable robot capable of operating both on the ground and in the air. Inspired by recent work from Caltech on ATMO-like robotic systems.

2-DOF Bicopter for Thesis

Ongoing thesis project involving the design and control of a bicopter platform intended for both research and teaching advanced control concepts.

ALIP-Based Control for Bipedal Robotics

Independent project studying and implementing the Angular Linear Inverted Pendulum (ALIP) controller proposed by Gong and Grizzle, focusing on step-to-step dynamics and simplified models for bipedal locomotion.
